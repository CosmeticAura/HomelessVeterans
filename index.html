Brief Overview:

Homeless Veterans is an enduring issue that the United States has faced since WW1. As of 2024 32,882 Veterans have experienced homelessness making up 5% of al homeless adults in the United States. Most Homeless Veterans served in wars like WW1, WW2, Korean War, Vietnam,  Afghanistan, and the Iraq War. These brutal war caused many mental health issues to veterans like PTSD, substance abuse, and TBI. This made it hard for Veterans to find jobs eventually leading to homelessness.

Wars that Caused Homeless Veteran

WW2 (1939-1945): 
World War II, one of the deadliest conflicts in Global History, soldiers who fought in this war witnessed many atrocity's, mass destruction and death. Many soldiers came home with mental issues like PTSD. At the time PTSD was not recognized as a condition, leaving thousands of veterans suffering. In 1944, the GI Bill act was passed helping many veterans with housing and education for many it helped a lot. Unfortunately, Black Veterans were denied the benefits of the GI Bill act due to racial discrimination in 1944 leaving many without support resulting in homelessness. Most WW2 veterans now have passed or are elderly. Veterans who did not receive benefits struggled there entire life.

Korean War (1950-1953):
The Korean War often known as the "Forgotten War" because unlike other wars when soldiers came home to a society that rarely talked about it. Veterans from the Korean War had little recognition or support. Veterans like other wars came home with PTSD which was still not recognized as a condition, so veterans were not treated. To cope with PTSD many turned to alcohol or drugs to cope, this lead to job loss and eventually homelessness. The economy in the US during the 1970s were very bad inflation rose along with unemployment making it even harder for Veterans.

Vietnam War (1955-1975):
The Vietnam War produced the most amount of homeless veterans in US history. Unlike any other wars soldiers came back to a country that was angry about the war, resulting in many Veterans not getting support or recognition. Most veterans like other wars suffered from PTSD which was still not recognized as a mental health issue. Many veterans  turned to drugs and alcohol to cope resulting in job loss and eventually leading to homelessness. Jobs were also hard to find in the 1970s because there was a rise of inflation and unemployment . Military skills also did not transfer to regular jobs making it even harder to find a job. With PTSD, substance abuse, not have skills of a regular job, and the rise of inflation and unemployment combined in made it very hard to find a job a make a living causing the biggest wave of veteran homelessness during this time.

Iraq War (2003-2011):
Like other wars many veterans came back with PTSD and TBI from  IED explosions.
Many of Iraq veterans ended up in shelter after returning home. PTSD directly led to homelessness, PTSD made it hard to maintain jobs and caused many to turn to drugs and alcohol to cope. Veterans received more support than other wars like Vietnam when coming home. Many veterans had shelters to stay at instead of the streets.

Afghanistan (2001-2021):
 Veterans who came came back from the Afghanistan War returned with PTSD and TBI (traumatic brain injury) from constant combat stress and IED explosion. These conditions were hard to treat because they were recently recognized. Because of rare treatment many veterans struggled to readjust to normal life. Without proper treatment many veterans turned to drugs and alcohol to deal with trauma from the War. This left many vulnerable to homelessness.

Causes of Homeless Veterans

-Untreated mental health issues (PTSD)

-Used drugs and alcohol to cope

-difficulty finding jobs

- Lack of affordable housing

-lack of support for homeless vets

Causes of Homelessness:

- Many Veterans had PTSD. PTSD was not recognized as a mental health issue, so most were left untreated

- Substance Abuse: Many veterans turned to alcohol or drugs to cope with PTSD or trauma. Addiction leads to job loss, eventually leading to homelessness.

-Difficulty Finding Jobs: Military skills did not transfer to regular jobs. Most veterans had a hard time finding jobs after leaving the military.

Government Programs, Solutions, and Improvements

-HUD-VASH (Housing and Urban Development Veterans Affairs Supportive Housing) was a government program that helped veterans with rent. It would give veterans rental vouchers that could be used to rent a apartment in there community. Services were also provided to these veterans. This helped a lot with homeless veterans. It has served many homeless veterans helping them get back on there feet.


-SSVF (Supportive Services for Veterans and Families) try's to prevent homeless veterans before it happens. It helps veterans who are about to lose there homes. The program is aimed to quickly rehouse veterans. 

-In 3 states, 85 communities have announced an end to Veteran homelessness a big step to stop homeless veterans entirely

How has Homeless Veterans influenced the shaping of Global History in the 20th Century and Beyond?

Homeless Veterans have played a significant role in shaping global history in the 20th century and beyond in many way. Homeless Veterans exposed the true cost of war. They showed how my caused suffering and homelessness. Veterans suffered for years after war with many mental conditions like PSTD and TBI leading to substance abuse to cope. This greatly effected veterans and caused many waves of homeless veterans that still exist today. Homeless veterans also changed mental health care for the entire world. For most of time, many veterans suffered from mental illnesses like PTSD this condition affected many veterans around the world because it was not treated or recognized and it led to substance abuse to cope eventually leading to homelessness. Since so many veterans were struggled the the recognition of PTSD was forced. This helped treat many veterans, disaster survivor, and abuse victims that suffered from PTSD. Lastly the crisis of Homeless veterans forced to create many programs for homelessness like HUD-VASH. This helped solve homelessness and made it decrease overtime.

Take Action

How You Can Help

Veteran homelessness is a problem that can be solved . It starts with everyday people taking action. Here are some way you can help stop homeless veterans.

Donate

You can donate to charities that help homeless veterans. One example NCHV (National Coalition for Homeless Veterans) a nonprofit organization that provides emergency and supportive housing, food, health services, and job training.

Volunteer

You can Volunteer at local homeless veteran shelters. You can help many ways in the shelter, you can give out food talk to the veterans and help them.

Spread awareness

You can spread awareness through social media. Make informational videos tell people to help or donate.

Hire veterans

If you own a business you can gives opportunities to veterans by giving jobs. This will help them earn a steady salary to have a home. Programs like Hire Heroes USA can help you hire veterans.
